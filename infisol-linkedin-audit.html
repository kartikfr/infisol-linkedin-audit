What's Not Working

Six issues behind the 4.5 score

Critical

Hindi B2C Videos Are the Wrong Format for LinkedIn

Five posts, average 21 reactions, zero comments. "Ab Bill Nahi Karega Bother" (22R), "Factory ka electricity bill" (17R), "Solar decision delay ho sakta hai" (17R), "Sabse mehenga business decision" (26R), "AC chalao ya bill se daro" (25R). High production effort: animated graphics, AI visuals, dramatic music. Zero business result. The LinkedIn audience is B2B: procurement managers and industrial buyers, not rooftop B2C consumers. These formats belong on Instagram Reels and YouTube Shorts.

Fix

Pull all Hindi B2C promo videos from the LinkedIn calendar. Move them to Instagram or YouTube Shorts. Replace with English B2B content: buyer education, policy commentary, project proof.

Critical

Voice Fragments Across Three Registers

The page sounds like three companies. Utility Project Series posts are precise ("3.0 MW Solar Plant, Ghatanji, Yavatmal. Successfully commissioned."). Hindi promo videos are emotional and informal. Culture posts are casual. A buyer who lands on the page has no way to form a consistent impression. There is no unified voice.

Fix

One voice rule: "Informed insider." A senior project director speaking to professionals who care about specifics. Start with a fact, a number, or a question a commercial buyer would stop for. No exclamation marks. No dramatic openers that any competitor can copy.

Critical

No Posting Cadence

The post log shows 5 to 6 posts in a 2 to 3 day window, then silence for a week or more. LinkedIn rewards consistent, spaced posting and penalizes erratic bursts. Batch posting inflates impressions briefly but suppresses reach per post because the audience cannot engage before the next post takes the same attention slot.

Fix

4 to 5 posts per week, minimum 24 hours between posts. Tuesday, Wednesday, Thursday are peak days in India's professional market. Plan the week's posts in advance so every slot has a brief and a hook before Monday.

Critical

CTAs Are Missing on the Posts That Matter

Kusum series (194R, 24C), Ghatanji project (148R, 9C), Saundane project (89R, 6C). Three of the five best-performing posts have no call to action. The audience engaged and was given no next step. Hindi promo videos run "Call now" on content that has earned zero trust. CTAs and content stage are completely mismatched.

Match CTA to content stage
1
Project proof posts: "Considering a utility or rooftop project in Maharashtra? DM us for a capacity and timeline estimate."
2
Opinion / industry posts: "What's your read? Drop it in the comments." or "Share this with your energy procurement team."
3
Lead-gen posts only: careers@infisol-energy.in or infisol-energy.in. No phone numbers in top-of-funnel posts.
High Priority

Hashtag Overload Suppresses Distribution

Most posts carry 10 to 15 hashtags, including generic ones: #RenewableEnergy, #CleanEnergy, #SolarEnergy, #Sustainability. LinkedIn flags posts with more than 3 to 5 hashtags as broadcast content and reduces organic reach. Generic hashtags target no one. This single issue is cutting distribution on every post.

Fix

Cut to 3 to 5 hashtags per post. Core set: #SolarEPC, #MaharashtraSolar, #UtilitySolar, #RooftopSolar, #InfisolEnergy. Drop all generic sustainability tags.

High Priority

Festival and Observance Posts Generate No Conversation

Yoga Day (23R, 0C) and World Environment Day (28R, 0C). Both are interchangeable with any other solar company's observance post. Every solar EPC on LinkedIn runs the same format. At 7.5% of posts, each one displaces a project post that averages 97 reactions.

Fix

One per month maximum. Tie it to InfiSol's actual numbers. "InfiSol has offset X tonnes of CO2 across 150+ MWp in Maharashtra" beats a generic "Choose Solar" graphic every time.
